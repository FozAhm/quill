about

WarHacks is a 12-hour hackathon on Sunday March 17, 2019 at the Concordia Conference Centre. All college students and high school seniors are eligible to apply and attend. In teams of four participants will learn how to code and design a robot and then get an opportunity to make their own custom robot which will compete against robots from other teams in a final challenge. Students from any field of study are welcome as no experience is necessary to attend. The team whose robot is the most successful will win an award and a cool prize to take home!

faq

What is a hackathon?
Although many people associate the word hacking with illegal activities, a hackathon is completely the opposite. A hackathon is an event where people can come together and share their ideas to create something fun while also learning and having a great time.
Do I need a team?
Participants are expected to work in teams of four. If you already have a team you can indicate your team name and members when registering. But don’t worry if you don’t have a team, there will be a team building session where you can meet other participants and form a team with cool new people.
I have never been to a hackathon before, what should I do?
Do not worry if this is your first time, hackathons are a great place to learn and make new friends. Our hackathon is designed for beginners, so we’ll be there at every step of the way. As long as you’re ready to learn you will have a great time!
When and where is the event?
Warhacks takes place Sunday March 17, 2019 at the Concordia University Conference Centre located on the 9 th floor of the John Molson Building at the Sir George Williams Campus.
What should I bring?
We don’t ask you to bring anything because we will be providing you with all the equipment you need. However, it is suggested that if you intend to program, you bring your own laptop already set up with the Arduino IDE so that you can immediately start coding during the competition. You are also welcome to bring any hardware you want to experiment with at the hackathon as long as it’s safe; contact us if you are unsure.
What is the code of conduct?
We expect all participants to be respectful to everyone and follow all the rules of the event which can be found here.
Can I participate?
If you are a college student or a high school senior, then you are eligible to attend! No experience is necessary to participate in this hackathon. Just remember, if you are a minor on the day of the competition you will have get consent from a parent/guardian.
How much does it cost?
The event is completely free to attend. We want to make sure that anyone and everyone can attend. Just remember transportation to and from our hackathon venue is still your responsibility. If you have any concerns reach out to us!
